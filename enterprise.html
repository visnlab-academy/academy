Enterprise Architecture Course Modules

Module 1: Foundations of Enterprise Architecture

    1. Introduction to Enterprise Architecture (EA): Definition, purpose, and benefits
    2. Core principles of EA: Business alignment, stakeholder engagement, and continuous improvement
    3. Popular EA frameworks: TOGAF, Zachman Framework, The Open Group Architecture Framework (TOGAF)
    4. Comparison of different EA methodologies: Waterfall, Agile, Iterative
    5. Case studies: Exploring how successful organizations leverage EA principles

Module 2: Business Architecture

    1. Define Enterprise Architecture in your own words.
    2. Analyze a real-world scenario and identify how EA can be applied.
    3. Research and compare different EA frameworks.
    4. Develop a high-level business capability model for a specific industry.
    5. Analyze a case study where BA misalignment led to challenges.
    6. Identify KPIs for a chosen business process improvement initiative.

Module 3: Enterprise Information Architecture (EIA)

  1. Introduction to EIA: Managing, integrating, and governing enterprise data
  2. Data modeling techniques: Entity-Relationship Diagrams (ERDs)
  3. Data governance framework: Policies, roles, and responsibilities for data management
  4. Data quality management: Ensuring data accuracy and consistency
  5. Data integration strategies: Breaking down data silos for better insights

Module 4: Designing and Implementing Robust IT Solutions

  1. Defining ETA: Selecting and integrating hardware, software, and network infrastructure
  2. Application architecture: Layered architecture patterns (MVC, SOA)
  3. Technology selection and vendor management strategies
  4. Cloud computing and its impact on enterprise architecture
  5. Designing for scalability, security, and resilience
